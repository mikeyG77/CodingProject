C A S

The International Baccalaureate (IB) program has a component called CAS, which stands for Creativity, Activity, and Service. It is an essential part of IB designed to create a well-rounded student who will have a better developed personality and experiences afterwards.

Creativity

Creativity in CAS should make the student engage in creativive endeavors such as music, art, designing, etc. This is so that the student may develop new skills and expand their horizons academically and personally.

Activity

Activity in CAS should make the student engage with activities which promote a healthy lifestyle.

Service

Service in CAS should make the student contribute to their community by volunteering and engaging with organizations or experiences that adress potential problems within the community or that will a positive impact on the community.

Learning Outcomes |

As part of the reflection section of CAS, which will be explained afterwards, you must show evidence of each of the 7 learning outcomes. These learning objectives should be shown within your reflections. Not every learning objective will be found in each experience, however, you should show several instances where they appear within your CAS portfolio.

Strength and Growth

Identify own strengths and develop areas for growth.

Challenge and Skills

Demonstrate that challenges have been undertaken, developing new skills in the process.

Initiative and Planning

Demonstrate how to initiate and plan a CAS experience.

Commitment and Perseverance

Show commitment to, and perseverance in, CAS experiences.

Collaborative Skills

Demonstrate the skills and recognize the benefits of working collaboratively.

Global Engagement

Demonstrate engagement with issues of global significance.

Ethics of Choices and Actions

Recognize and consider the ethics of choices and actions.
